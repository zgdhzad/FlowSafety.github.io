Team Story

We are committed to solving global flood risk problems and reducing losses and casualties through technological innovation

Starting Point: Why We Started

Today, approximately 1.5 billion people worldwide are directly exposed to significant flood risk, and in 2024 alone, water-related disasters globally caused approximately 8,700 deaths and affected over 40 million lives.

In flood fatalities, 75% of deaths are caused by drowning. Existing solutions have lag and roughness, and the industry still lacks a relatively complete and systematic solution.

We found that in flood disasters, the lack of timely rescue due to information delays and the lack of personal awareness for autonomous risk avoidance are often overlooked, leading to many unfortunate deaths. We hope to help more people gain life-saving time through this project.

Mission: What We Solve Daily

Through AI models, feature recognition, and computer visualization, we enable every person in the city to know their flood risk earlier and more specifically, and to receive rescue and assistance in time.

We focus on transforming complex data into intuitive visual information, providing accurate and timely flood risk predictions for individuals, rescue agencies, and governments.

Vision: The World After Success

When we succeed, in the face of floods, people in the world will scientifically improve the speed of rescue decision-making, reduce the probability of drowning and other casualties, greatly improve people's safety awareness, and more people will devote themselves to solving safety issues of floods and other disasters. Cities will be endowed with sensing capabilities and responsibility boundaries.

We believe technology can give cities "sensing capabilities" to proactively respond to natural disasters and protect every resident.

Values: How We Work

We are guided by human needs, turning data and analysis into warmth. When analyzing risks, we focus on considering human situations and circumstances, turning abstract indicators into warm helpers.

We believe real situations are more important than ideal models. If the model does not match real events, we correct the model to align with real standards.

Even if the short-term impact may not be significant, we are still willing to persist in promotion and innovation. We focus on long-term impact and continuously promote technological innovation and application.

Core Innovations

Significant innovative breakthroughs in three levels: algorithm fusion, risk modeling, and application interaction

1

Multi-source Perception Fusion for "Local Flood Risk Assessment"

We not only perform water segmentation or person/vehicle detection, but simultaneously integrate:

  • Water area spatial distribution (semantic segmentation)
  • Person/vehicle precise location (YOLO)
  • Water depth estimation (visual + physics-inspired)
  • Terrain slope & water flow direction
  • Rainfall intensity (visual/external data)
  • Time extrapolation (risk in future N minutes)

This is "multi-modal risk modeling," not a simple object detection project.

2

Differentiated Flood Risk Modeling for "Pedestrians vs. Vehicles"

We achieve semantic risk assessment, not simple numerical threshold judgment:

Traditional Method

Generic standard of "water depth > X = danger"

Our Method

For people: ankle/knee/waist/chest different danger levels

For vehicles: tire/chassis/window different danger levels

For example: 0.4m water depth is fatal to a person but passable for an SUV; 0.6m water depth floods a sedan's engine, but a truck can still move slowly.

3

Dual-state Risk Determination: "In-Water" vs. "Near-Water"

We distinguish two situations:

  • Already in water: Calculate overlap ratio, directly enter submersion risk model
  • Not yet in water but close: Calculate distance to water, combine with nearest water depth for warning

Innovation Summary: Upgrading from "whether submerged" to "whether about to enter a dangerous situation," achieving true risk prediction rather than post-event analysis.

4

Sensor-independent "Pure Vision Flood Risk System"

Our system does not need water level gauges, radar, or IoT deployment, only needs:

  • Ordinary cameras
  • Existing surveillance/dashcams/drones

Practical Significance: During floods, sensors are most likely to be damaged, while cameras are most abundant, achieving a "low-cost + high coverage" solution.

5

Low-computation Water Depth Inference Based on "Semantic Anchors"

"Using humans as rulers": Uses YOLO-Pose to extract human skeleton keypoints (ankle, knee, hip), and directly determines physical water depth through the topological relationship between keypoints and water mask (e.g., "water over knees").

"Using vehicles as markers": Uses vehicle bounding box and water surface geometric intersection ratio to infer submersion level.

Advantages: No depth sensors needed, low computation, strong physical interpretability.

6

Role-adaptive Dual-view Decision Support System

User View

Emphasizes self-perception ("YOU" marker)

Intuitive dashboard and action advice

Focuses on survival rate

Rescue View

Emphasizes overall situation

Dual-graph comparison (current vs. future)

Focuses on resource allocation

Transforms computer vision results into effective intelligence for different groups, enhancing system practical application value.
